# Organisation Prospect Profile Template

## Purpose

Provide a separate profile format for organisations, companies, trusts, foundations, and partners.

## Default

Use the standard profile unless the user requests a short or comprehensive organisation profile.

## Operating Principle

This is not an individual profile with the name swapped out. Organisation profiles should focus on structure, leadership, activity, capacity, partnership relevance, and confirmed institutional relationship. Do not infer a UC connection from location, sector, shared interests, or plausible relevance.

## Profile Types

| Format | Length | Use when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Short | 250-450 words | Quick briefing on a company, trust, foundation, or partner. |
| Standard | 800-1,300 words | Default organisation profile for UC Development use. |
| Comprehensive | 1,600+ words | Deeper research including capacity indicators, due diligence, and detailed evidence notes. |

## Agent-Facing Section Map

| Section | Status | Typical length | Agent instruction |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Snapshot | Required | 2-4 sentences | Identify what the organisation is, where it operates, why it may matter, and the strongest confirmed context. |
| Organisation overview | Required | 1-3 paragraphs | Summarise purpose, sector, operating model, products or services, location, scale, and current public position. |
| Leadership and key people | Recommended | 1-2 paragraphs | List relevant leaders, directors, founders, owners, trustees, or decision-makers. Include UC links only where confirmed. |
| Confirmed UC connection | Optional | Brief note | Include only confirmed links such as partnership, research collaboration, sponsorship, recruitment, alumni leaders, prior giving, events, or user-provided context. |
| Philanthropy, sponsorship, and community activity | Optional | 1-3 paragraphs | Summarise public giving, sponsorships, foundation activity, scholarships, CSR, volunteering, or community partnerships. |
| Sector position and influence | Recommended | 1-2 paragraphs | Describe market position, regional or national relevance, industry influence, policy presence, networks, or reputation. |
| Commercial, research, or partnership relevance | Optional | 1-2 paragraphs | Capture factual relevance to UC activity such as research, student pathways, graduate recruitment, innovation, or regional development. |
| Capacity and resourcing indicators | Comprehensive | 1-2 paragraphs | Use supportable public indicators only: revenue bands, employee count, ownership, assets, contracts, funding, filings, or scale. |
| Sensitivities and due diligence | Comprehensive | Brief note | Include only well-supported, directly relevant, and necessary legal, reputational, regulatory, ethical, governance, or financial matters. |
| Source notes | Recommended | Short bullets | Summarise source types, recency, name ambiguity, ownership uncertainty, missing filings, and limits on confidence. |

## Blank Template

### Snapshot

[Organisation] is a [type of organisation] operating in [sector/location]. Its relevance to UC Development is [brief confirmed reason or public context]. The strongest supported points are [scale, leadership, community activity, partnership context, sector relevance].

### Organisation Overview

[Describe purpose, sector, operating model, major products/services, markets, locations, current public position, and any relevant recent developments.]

### Leadership and Key People

[Summarise relevant senior leaders, directors, founders, trustees, owners, or key decision-makers. Include confirmed UC links only. Do not imply institutional connection through a person unless it is explicit.]

### Confirmed UC Connection

[Include confirmed links such as partnerships, research collaboration, sponsorship, recruitment, event involvement, prior giving, alumni leaders, or known staff relationships. If none is confirmed, use: No confirmed UC connection identified in the material reviewed.]

### Philanthropy, Sponsorship, and Community Activity

[Summarise confirmed giving, sponsorships, scholarships, foundation activity, CSR programmes, volunteering, nonprofit partnerships, and community initiatives.]

### Sector Position and Partnership Relevance

[Describe market position, regional/national relevance, industry influence, public reputation, and factual relevance to UC activity such as research, student pathways, internships, graduate recruitment, innovation, or regional development.]

### Capacity and Resourcing Indicators

[Comprehensive profiles only unless requested. Include supportable indicators such as revenue bands, employee count, ownership structure, assets, major contracts, funding, filings, or scale of operations. Avoid unsupported assumptions.]

### Sensitivities and Due Diligence

[Comprehensive profiles only, or if specifically requested. Include material legal, reputational, ethical, regulatory, environmental, financial, or governance issues only where well-supported and directly relevant.]

### Source Notes

[Briefly list source types used and major limitations, such as dated annual reports, unclear ownership, incomplete leadership information, or name ambiguity.]

## Fictional Example

### Profile: Harakeke Health Technologies

Fictional organisation created for format testing only.

### Snapshot

Harakeke Health Technologies is a fictional Christchurch-based medical technology company developing remote monitoring tools for community and rural health providers. The example demonstrates the intended profile style: factual, concise, organisation-focused, and careful not to imply a UC relationship unless one is confirmed.

### Organisation Overview

In this fictional scenario, Harakeke Health Technologies designs software-enabled monitoring devices and clinical dashboards used by primary care networks. It operates from Christchurch, employs a specialist team across engineering, clinical design, and implementation support, and has pilot activity with several South Island health providers.

The organisation's public position is strongest in health technology, regional innovation, and applied clinical tools. A real profile should prioritise current operations and evidence of scale over a full company history.

### Leadership and Key People

The fictional leadership team includes a chief executive with a biomedical engineering background, a chief clinical adviser formerly attached to a district health service, and an independent chair with governance experience in early-stage technology companies. No UC connection is assigned to these individuals unless confirmed by source material.

### Confirmed UC Connection

No confirmed UC connection identified in the material reviewed. In a real profile, this section should not be replaced with speculative links based on geography, sector relevance, likely alumni connections, or possible research overlap.

### Philanthropy, Sponsorship, and Community Activity

Example material describes the company supporting a local youth health innovation challenge and providing in-kind technical mentoring to a community STEM programme. These are framed as public community activities, not as evidence of philanthropic capacity beyond what the sources support.

### Sector Position and Partnership Relevance

Harakeke would be relevant to UC from a sector perspective because of its fictional activity in health technology, engineering design, software, and regional innovation. This is partnership relevance, not a confirmed relationship. Potential overlap with student pathways, applied research, or industry engagement should only be stated as factual context unless a partnership is confirmed.

### Capacity and Resourcing Indicators

In this fictional comprehensive example, supportable indicators would include employee range, product deployment scale, investment announcements, public contracts, or filed financial information. No revenue or valuation estimate is included because the example does not provide a credible public basis for one.

### Sensitivities and Due Diligence

No material sensitivities are identified in the fictional example. A real profile should only include this section where the information is well-supported and directly relevant.

### Source Notes

This example is entirely fictional and should not be treated as a research finding. In real use, source notes should identify the source types reviewed, whether information is current, and any uncertainty around ownership, leadership, scale, or identity.
