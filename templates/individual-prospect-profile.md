# Individual Prospect Profile Template

## Purpose

Provide a clean, modular profile format that can contract or expand based on available evidence.

## Default

Use the standard profile unless the user requests a short or detailed profile.

## Operating Principle

Sections are optional, but evidence standards are not. If a section cannot be supported by credible public or user-provided material, omit it or state the limitation briefly. Do not add recommendations, suggested next steps, action items, or engagement strategy unless explicitly requested.

## Profile Lengths

| Format | Length | Use when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Short | 250-400 words | Senior leader preparation, event context, or quick briefing. |
| Standard | 700-1,200 words | Default prospect profile for UC Development use. |
| Detailed | 1,500+ words | Deeper internal research with fuller evidence notes and caveats. |

## Agent-Facing Section Map

| Section | Status | Typical length | Agent instruction |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Snapshot | Required | 2-4 sentences | Identify who the person is, why they are relevant, and the strongest supported advancement context. |
| Current roles and public profile | Optional | 1-3 paragraphs | Use confirmed current roles first, then significant board, governance, civic, or sector positions. |
| Connection to UC | Optional | 1-2 paragraphs | Include alumni, staff, faculty, event, research, giving, governance, or relationship links only when supported. |
| Philanthropy and community involvement | Optional | 1-3 paragraphs | Include confirmed giving, foundation, trust, nonprofit, volunteering, and public campaign involvement. |
| Professional and wealth indicators | Optional | 1-2 paragraphs | Use cautious language and only public indicators such as senior roles, ownership, directorships, filings, or credible public lists. |
| Interests and alignment | Optional | 1-2 paragraphs | Include only for detailed/comprehensive profiles or when explicitly requested. Map public interests to UC priorities without overstating affinity. |
| Due diligence and sensitivities | Optional | Brief note | Include only well-supported, directly relevant, and necessary sensitivity or reputational context. |
| Evidence notes and caveats | Optional | Short bullets | Include mainly for detailed/comprehensive profiles or when requested. Summarise source quality, unresolved ambiguity, dated information, and confidence limits. |

## Blank Template

### Snapshot

[Name] is [current role / descriptor]. Their relevance to UC Development is [brief confirmed reason or public context]. The strongest supported points are [connection, philanthropy, leadership, alignment, or other evidence-backed context].

### Current Roles and Public Profile

[Summarise current role, organisation, sector, board/governance roles, public profile, and relevant career context. Lead with current facts rather than full chronology.]

### Connection to UC

[Describe confirmed UC connection, such as alumni status, faculty link, event participation, staff relationship, prior giving, governance, research connection, or family/organisation connection. Omit if unsupported, or state: No confirmed UC connection identified in the material reviewed.]

### Philanthropy and Community Involvement

[Summarise confirmed charitable giving, trust/foundation activity, nonprofit board roles, volunteering, civic service, campaign involvement, or public community support. Separate facts from inference.]

### Professional and Wealth Indicators

[Summarise public capacity indicators cautiously. Include business ownership, senior executive responsibilities, directorships, public filings, property/business indicators, or credible public wealth references only if relevant and supportable.]

### Interests and Alignment

[Use only when requested or when the profile type calls for it. Map supported public interests to UC priorities, faculties, research areas, student support, regional development, equity, innovation, health, arts, sport, or community themes. Avoid unsupported claims of affinity.]

### Due Diligence and Sensitivities

[Include only if well-supported, directly relevant, and necessary. Use neutral wording and note uncertainty. Omit if no material sensitivities are identified.]

### Evidence Notes and Caveats

[Use when requested or for detailed profiles. Keep brief. Note source types used, source dates, name ambiguity, unavailable information, and confidence limits. Keep donor-level data, raw IDs, and unnecessary methodology out of the final briefing.]

## Fictional Example

### Profile: Dr Eleanor Marsh

Fictional individual created for format testing only.

### Snapshot

Dr Eleanor Marsh is a Christchurch-based technology executive and former biomedical engineer whose public work sits at the intersection of health innovation, governance, and regional entrepreneurship. She is a fictional example for template testing, but the profile demonstrates the intended style: concise, sourced in tone, cautious about inference, and suitable for an internal advancement briefing.

### Current Roles and Public Profile

Marsh is described in this example as founder and chief executive of Koru Diagnostics, a medium-sized medical technology company developing point-of-care testing tools for rural clinics. She is also presented as a director of the Canterbury Health Innovation Forum and a member of a national advisory group on digital health adoption.

Her public profile is strongest in health technology, applied research commercialisation, and regional innovation. The most briefing-relevant detail is not a long career history, but her current visibility across health, business, and research-facing networks.

### Connection to UC

In this fictional scenario, Marsh completed a Bachelor of Engineering with Honours at UC and later contributed to a panel discussion hosted by the Faculty of Engineering. She has no confirmed recent giving in the example material, and that absence should not be interpreted as a lack of interest.

### Philanthropy and Community Involvement

Example public material links Marsh to a health equity scholarship fund administered by a local community foundation and to pro bono advisory work for a youth STEM mentoring programme. These activities suggest a public pattern of interest in education access, health outcomes, and practical support for early-career talent.

### Professional and Wealth Indicators

The strongest fictional capacity indicators are company ownership, senior executive responsibility, directorships, and involvement in a venture-backed sector. No specific wealth estimate is included, because the example does not provide a credible public basis for one.

### Interests and Alignment

Marsh's public activity would align most naturally with UC themes in engineering, health technology, student scholarships, entrepreneurship, and research translation. Her fictional community work also suggests possible alignment with widening participation in STEM, although that should be framed as an observed theme rather than a confirmed philanthropic preference.

### Due Diligence and Sensitivities

No material reputational concerns are identified in the fictional example. A real profile should only include this section where the information is well-supported, directly relevant, and necessary for appropriate context.

### Evidence Notes and Caveats

This example is entirely fictional and should not be treated as a research finding. In real use, evidence notes should identify the source types used, the recency of key material, any name ambiguity, and any unsupported areas that were deliberately left out.
